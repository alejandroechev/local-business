## 1. 🎲 BOARD GAME CAFÉ / LUDOTECA

**Concept:** Café with 200+ board game library, cover charge + food/drink revenue, hosting game nights, tournaments, and D&D sessions.

| Factor | Details |
|---|---|
| **CAPEX** | **$46,000–$76,000 USD** (CLP 44M–73M). Mid-range: ~$57,000. Includes game library ($8K–10K), café equipment ($5K–8K), renovation ($8K–16K), furniture ($4K–6K), working capital. |
| **Monthly OPEX** | **$6,500–$11,000 USD**. Rent CLP 1.8M–2.2M, staff (4–6 ppl) CLP 2.4M–3.8M, F&B COGS ~30% of sales |
| **Revenue Model** | Cover: CLP 4,000–6,000/person (~$4–6). F&B average ~CLP 5,000/person. Events: CLP 25K–40K/table. Annual est: ~$104K–$115K USD |
| **Space** | 120–150 m² recommended |
| **Staffing** | 4 (low season) to 6 (high season). Baristas + game hosts |
| **Pros for PV** | ★ ZERO competition in ALL of southern Chile. German heritage = board game culture (Eurogames like Catan originated in Germany). 178 rainy days = captive market. Bariloche (Argentina, near-identical town profile) has this working. Tourist + local appeal year-round. |
| **Cons/Risks** | Imported games are 20–40% more expensive in Chile. Seasonal revenue swing (55% in 4 summer months). Game theft/damage. |
| **Competition** | **None in Los Lagos region.** 8–15 ludotecas in Santiago (El Tablero, Spieltisch, Mabanga). Nearest competitor ~1,000 km away. |
| **Examples** | Draughts (Edinburgh), Café Spilabúðin (Reykjavik), game bars in Bariloche — all in rainy tourist towns |
| **Feasibility** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5) |

# 📋 KEY CROSS-CUTTING FINDINGS

1. **ZERO indoor entertainment businesses exist in Puerto Varas/Puerto Montt** for any of these 14 categories. Every single concept is a regional first-mover.

2. **178 rainy days/year is the single strongest market driver.** Every concept benefits from bad weather pushing tourists and locals indoors.

3. **German heritage is a double-edged sword.** The culture supports board games and European-style social activities, but the café/food market is already saturated by 140+ years of German bakeries and restaurants. Don't compete on heritage food — compete on experiences.

4. **Pop-up/lean models dramatically reduce risk.** Murder mystery dinner (pop-up with restaurant partner) and art workshop can launch for under $30K combined, proving demand before larger investments.

5. **The escape room + puzzle room + rage room combo is the proven Chilean model.** 60 No More in Santiago literally operates a rage room inside their escape room venue. This is the playbook.

6. **Tourism seasonality is manageable.** The 4 summer months (Dec–Mar) generate ~55% of annual revenue. Off-season strategies: local events, school groups, corporate team building, digital nomad meetups, loyalty programs.
